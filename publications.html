WACV 2026 · IEEE/CVF Conference

One-cycle Structured Pruning with Stability Driven Subnetwork Search

Introduces a one-cycle structured pruning strategy that searches for stable subnetworks while the model is being compressed. The goal is to make deep vision models lighter and more deployable without relying on a long, multi-stage pruning pipeline.

Expand
Venue
Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision (WACV), 2026, pp. 5467-5476.
Authors
Deepak Ghimire, Dayoung Kil, Seonghwan Jeong, Jaesik Park, Seong-heum Kim
Affiliations
IT Application Research Center, Korea Electronics Technology Institute; Department of Intelligent Semiconductors, Soongsil University; Department of Computer Science & Engineering, Seoul National University.
Abstract Summary
The paper introduces a one-cycle structured pruning framework that combines pre-training, pruning, and fine-tuning into a single training cycle. It searches for stable subnetworks during early training using group saliency, structured sparsity regularization, and a stability indicator based on subnetwork similarity across epochs. Experiments on CIFAR-10/100 and ImageNet with VGGNet, ResNet, and MobileNet show competitive accuracy with strong training-time efficiency.
